Parent Agreement:

In consideration of ILSC accepting my child as a caregiving student, I agree to indemnify the school, its officers, and employees, against any claims, damages claims or demands arising from illness, accident, or death involving my child during their study period

I authorize any school staff member in the event of illness or accident, to obtain medical assistance or treatment of my child as necessary. This includes the administration of anaesthetic or blood transfusion if required. I authorize the school to engage doctors, nurses, or hospital services as needed, and I agree to pay all relevant medical and hospital costs.

I also agree to cover any reasonable communication expenses incurred by the school when contacting me or my family in connection with my child's welfare.

PARENT CONSENT FOR STUDENT INDEPENDENCE

I confirm that my child has permission to:

  • Travel independently between their host/caregiver's residence and the school campus using public transport.
  • Return home independently after daily classes or school activities.
  • Leave the school campus independently during lunch breaks to purchase food from nearby outlets.
  • Participate in all school-organised and supervised activities and excursions, including sports (e.g. soccer, rugby).

CAREGIVING CONTRACT FORM

OBLIGATIONS OF THE CAREGIVER/PARENT

The caregiver is responsible for providing appropriate supervision and support for the student both inside and outside of school during their stay in Australia. The caregiver must keep the parents informed of any issues or concerns that arise during the student's time in Australia and throughout their studies.

The respective roles of the caregiver and parent are outlined below.

School Attendance:
  • Ensure the student maintains a minimum attendance rate of 80% and arrives at class on time each day.
  • Notify the school at the start of the day if the student is absent or unwell.
  • Ensure the student is appropriately supervised at all times outside school hours, including during social and leisure activities.
Behaviour:
  • Ensure the student behaves appropriately at school and on school premises, in accordance with expectations for students under 18 years of age in Australia.
  • Provide regular feedback to the school and parents about any behavioural or conduct issues affecting the student's progress.
  • Ensure the student understands emergency procedures (including the 24-hour emergency number 0407 761 133), and is aware of Australian laws that apply to minors (under 18 years old).
Communication:
  • Communicate with ILSC staff in English at all times.
  • Attend teacher interviews and meetings when requested by the school.
  • Notify Student Services by email when requesting a leave of absence for the student (e.g. illness, medical appointment, family visit)
  • Attend any briefings or meetings as required by ILSC.
  • Sign all relevant documents, including excursion approvals.
  • Keep parents informed in writing of the student's living arrangements, travel plans, and excursion details.
  • Inform parents promptly of any issues or concerns regarding the student's welfare or behaviour.
Medical Attentions:

As the caregiver, you must assist the student in obtaining medical attention when necessary and ensure that a valid medical certificate is provided to the school for any absence due to illness.

If you are unable to provide emergency transport, the student's parents are responsible for any related costs (for example, taxi fares to medical appointments or to return to the homestay for rest).

  • Medical Emergencies: Contact the parents and the school immediately if the student is involved in an accident, becomes seriously ill, or requires urgent medical care.
  • Personal Assistance: Provide support to the student with any personal, cultural, or resettlement matters related to their stay and studies.
  • Contact Details: Ensure your contact details are up to date and shared with the student, parents, and school. Notify all parties promptly if any changes occur.
  • Availability: Remain available to the student and the school at all times in the event of an emergency.
